STREETSENTINEL – AI-BASED REAL-TIME URBAN SAFETY AND THREAT DETECTION SYSTEM
StreetSentinel is an AI-powered urban safety system designed to provide real-time automatic threat detection and emergency response using smartphones. The system consists of a Citizen App and a Police App connected through a cloud backend. The Citizen App continuously monitors environmental sounds using the smartphone microphone and AI-based sound classification models such as YAMNet to identify distress signals including screams, cries for help, or violent sounds. Upon detecting a potential threat, the application automatically triggers an emergency alert without requiring manual user interaction. The alert includes the user's live GPS location, timestamp, and emergency details, which are securely transmitted to the backend. The backend instantly forwards the alert to nearby police officers through the Police App, where incidents are displayed on a live map dashboard for rapid response. StreetSentinel transforms smartphones into intelligent safety sensors and provides a proactive, scalable, and privacy-focused solution for urban public safety.

THE FIELD OF INVENTION (HEALTHCARE INDUSTRY)
This invention pertains to the field of 5G-enabled computer vision and real-time video analytics, specifically crowd density estimation and monitoring using live camera feeds in urban infrastructure, public safety, and event management.
BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
Traditional crowd counting relies on manual observation or outdated infrared sensors, which suffer from inaccuracies in varying lighting, occlusions, and scalability issues. These methods fail to deliver real-time insights needed for safety in high-density areas like festivals, stadiums, or transit points. Conventional systems also lack high-speed connectivity, leading to delays in data processing and response. A need exists for a robust, low-latency solution that integrates 5G for seamless video streaming and AI for precise density estimation.
SUMMARY OF THE INVENTION
The 5G-Based Real-Time Crowd Counting Using Camera system simplifies crowd monitoring by capturing live video streams from IP cameras, transmitting data via 5G networks to edge servers for initial AI-based density estimation using attention models. Aggregated counts and analytics are displayed on a centralized web/mobile dashboard, enabling authorities to visualize heatmaps, track trends, and receive instant alerts for overcrowding thresholds. This architecture ensures ultra-low latency under 10ms, scalability for multi-camera deployments, and seamless integration without physical sensors.

THE FIELD OF INVENTION (PUBLIC SAFETY INDUSTRY)
This invention relates to AI-powered urban safety systems, real-time sound analysis, emergency response applications, and smart city public safety monitoring using smartphones and cloud-based communication systems.
BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
Existing emergency response solutions such as SOS applications and wearable panic devices depend heavily on manual user activation, which may not be possible during dangerous situations. CCTV systems mainly provide post-incident evidence without proactive intervention. Therefore, there is a need for an intelligent system capable of automatically detecting distress situations and immediately alerting nearby authorities in real time.
SUMMARY OF THE INVENTION
StreetSentinel provides an automated safety ecosystem using AI-based sound recognition, GPS tracking, and cloud communication. The Citizen App continuously monitors environmental sounds locally on the smartphone and automatically sends alerts when danger signals are detected. The Police App receives these alerts through a centralized backend and displays incidents on a live dashboard for immediate action. The system is scalable, cost-effective, privacy-focused, and does not require additional hardware.
